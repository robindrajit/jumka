1. Romantic Poetry?

"Romantic poetry is a genre that flourished in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, emphasizing emotion, imagination, and individualism."

Here's an example of a famous romantic poem:
O my Luve's like a red, red rose,
That's newly sprung in June:
O my Luve's like the melodie,
That's sweetly play'd in tune.

This poem by Robert Burns exemplifies the romantic tradition with its passionate expression of love and vivid imagery drawn from nature.

2. ODE?

"An ode is a type of lyrical poem that is usually addressed to a particular subject or person, often celebrating or praising them. Odes typically have a formal structure and lofty, elevated language."

Here's an example of a famous ode:
My heart aches, and a drowsy numbness pains
My sense, as though of hemlock I had drunk,

"Ode to a Nightingale" by John Keats is a classic example of an ode. In this poem, Keats addresses a nightingale, expressing his desire to escape the pain and suffering of life through the transcendent experience of listening to the bird's song. The poem is characterized by its rich imagery, emotional intensity, and exploration of themes such as mortality and the power of art.

3. Metaphysical?

"Metaphysical poetry is a poetic movement that emerged in the 17th century, characterized by intellectual exploration, complex conceits, and a focus on abstract and philosophical themes."

Here's an example of a metaphysical poem:
As virtuous men pass mildly away,
And whisper to their souls to go,
Whilst some of their sad friends do say
The breath goes now, and some say, No:

In "A Valediction: Forbidding Mourning," John Donne uses elaborate conceits and metaphors to explore the nature of love and separation.

4. Pastoral Poetry?

"Pastoral poetry is a genre that idealizes rural life and often depicts shepherds or other rural figures in a tranquil, natural setting.""

Here's an example of a famous pastoral poem:
Come live with me and be my love,
And we will all the pleasures prove,
That Valleys, groves, hills, and fields,
Woods, or steepy mountain yields.

In "The Passionate Shepherd to His Love," Christopher Marlowe presents an idyllic vision of rural life, inviting his beloved to join him in a pastoral paradise filled with natural beauty and simple pleasures.

5. Epicgram?

" An epigram is a short, witty, and often satirical poem or statement that expresses a clever idea or observation. "

Here's an example of an epigram:
I am his Highness' dog at Kew;
Pray tell me, sir, whose dog are you?

In this epigram by Alexander Pope, the speaker, a dog, humorously questions the status and identity of the person it is addressing.

6. Lyric Poetry?

"Lyric poetry is a genre that expresses personal emotions or feelings and is often musical in nature.""

Here's an example of a lyric poem:

And, in parting from you now,
Thus much let me avow —
You are not wrong, who deem

In "A Dream Within a Dream," Edgar Allan Poe explores the fleeting nature of reality and the human experience.

7. Narrative Poetry?

"Narrative poetry is a form of poetry that tells a story through verse. These poems often have a plot, characters, and a setting, and they unfold like a narrative or a short story."

Here's an example of a narrative poem:
Once upon a midnight dreary, while I pondered, weak and weary,
Over many a quaint and curious volume of forgotten lore—

"The Raven" by Edgar Allan Poe is a prime example of narrative poetry. In this poem, the speaker encounters a mysterious raven that enters his chamber and engages in a conversation with it, revealing the speaker's descent into madness and his obsession with his lost love, Lenore. The poem unfolds like a narrative, with a clear storyline, characters, and dialogue.

8.Epic?

"An epic is a long narrative poem that typically recounts the adventures and heroic deeds of a legendary or mythological figure."

Here's an example of a famous epic:
"The Odyssey" is an ancient Greek epic poem attributed to the poet Homer. It tells the story of Odysseus, King of Ithaca, and his journey home after the Trojan War. The poem is divided into 24 books and follows Odysseus as he faces numerous challenges, battles mythical creatures, and encounters gods and goddesses while trying to return to his homeland and his faithful wife, Penelope. "The Odyssey" is celebrated for its rich storytelling, vivid imagery, and exploration of themes such as heroism, loyalty, and the human experience.

9.Ballad

" A ballad is a narrative poem that typically tells a story in a simple and rhythmic manner."

Here's an example of a traditional ballad:
It is an ancient Mariner,
And he stoppeth one of three.
'By thy long grey beard and glittering eye,
Now wherefore stopp'st thou me?

"The Rime of the Ancient Mariner" by Samuel Taylor Coleridge is a classic example of a ballad. In this poem, a mariner tells a wedding guest the harrowing tale of his journey at sea, where he encounters supernatural events and learns the consequences of his actions. The poem's simple language, repetitive structure, and rhythmic cadence are characteristic of the ballad form.

10.Elegy?

"An elegy is a poem that mourns the loss of someone or something, often expressing sorrow, lamentation, or tribute. "

Here's an example of an elegy:
Strong Son of God, immortal Love,
Whom we, that have not seen thy face,
By faith, and faith alone, embrace,
Believing where we cannot prove;

Alfred, Lord Tennyson's "In Memoriam A.H.H." is one of the most famous elegies in the English language.

11.Sonnet?

"A sonnet is a 14-line poem with a specific rhyme scheme and meter. The most common forms are the Petrarchan (or Italian) sonnet and the Shakespearean (or English) sonnet. "

Here's an example of Shakespearean sonnet:
Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
Thou art more lovely and more temperate:

In Sonnet 18, Shakespeare uses the theme of nature's transience to compare the beauty of the poem's subject to a summer's day. He asserts that unlike the fleeting nature of summer, the beauty of the person he is addressing will be preserved forever through the poem itself. This sonnet follows the typical Shakespearean rhyme scheme of ABABCDCDEFEFGG, with each quatrain followed by a rhyming couplet.
